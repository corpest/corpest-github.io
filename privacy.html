Privacy Policy

Last updated: September 2025

1. Introduction

Madison Bot ("we", "us", or "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect information when you use our bot on Discord.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the minimum information necessary for the bot to function:

  • Discord user IDs
  • Server IDs where the bot is added
  • Command usage data for analytics and performance

3. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used exclusively for:

  • Providing and improving bot functionality
  • Debugging, analytics, and performance monitoring
  • Preventing abuse or misuse of the bot

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal information with third parties. Information may only be shared if required by law.

5. Data Storage & Security

All data is stored securely using industry-standard encryption and access controls. Data is automatically deleted after 30 days of inactivity. We take reasonable measures to protect your information but cannot guarantee complete security.

6. User Rights

You have the right to request information we hold about you or request deletion of your data. To exercise your rights, contact us via our GitHub repository or Discord.

7. Third-Party Services

The bot may use third-party services to operate, such as hosting and analytics tools. These services comply with their own privacy policies, and we do not share personal information with them beyond what is required for operation.

8. Children’s Privacy

Madison Bot does not knowingly collect personal information from users under the age of 13. If you are under 13, do not use the bot. We encourage parents to monitor children’s online activity.

9. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. Updated policies will be posted here with the new date. Continued use of the bot constitutes acceptance of any changes.

10. Contact

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please reach out via our GitHub repository or Discord.
